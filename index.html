Cooking less at home

A Response to a Social Shift

In 2015, the labor force consists of 46.8% women vs in 1948, only 28.6% are women.

Average Time Women Cook at Home


Women, who traditionally have carried the brunt of the cooking load,
are working more outside home, and therefore spending less time at home cooking.
In 2008, women spent 66 minutes per day cooking, almost 50 minutes
less than in the 1960s,when they spent upward of 112 minutes on average.

Changes in lifestyle?

What are the results of this new lifestyle

Researchers have found a positive correlation between dietary habits and body weight. Because of higher calorie count and poorer nutritional choice of meals and snacks, the rate of overweight and obesity is at record high.



What the BMI says:

Below 18.5: underweight
18.5-24.9: normal
25-29.9: overweight
30 or higher: obese


Nationally, nearly 68.8% of adults (ages 20+) are overweight or obese and nearly 8% are extremely obese. In 1950, only 10% of of adults were classified as obese.


Among youngsters (ages 2-19), about 31.8% are considered to be either overweight or obese.





By 2030, 51% of the population will be obese by 2030 if the rate of obesity stays consistent.


Obesity is linked to more than 60 chronic diseases including heart disease, stroke, some cancers, and type 2 diabetes.


The estimated annual health care costs of obesity-related illness are a staggering $190.2 billion, nearly 21% of annual medical spending.
